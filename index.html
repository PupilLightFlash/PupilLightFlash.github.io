WHO ARE WE SEEKING?

We are looking for HEALTHY MALE CHILDREN 2-18 YEARS of age who have no history of neurological, psychiatric, or
neuro-developmental diagnosis.

¿A QUIÉNES SOLICITAMOS?

Solicitamos la participación de varones sanos de 2 a 18 años de edad sin diagnósticos psiquiátricos, neurológicos o del desarrollo neurológico.

WHAT DOES IT INVOLVE?

Your child will be presented with moderate intensity light flashes on a computer screen while we take video of the pupil of his eye. You and your child will also fill out behavior assessment questionnaires that will be screened by a pediatrician.

¿EN QUÉ CONSISTE?

A su hijo se le presentarán destellos luminosos de intensidad moderada en una pantalla de ordenador mientras se graba un vídeo de su pupila. Usted y su hijo también rellenarán cuestionarios de comportamiento que serán revisados por un pediatra.

WHY IS IT IMPORTANT?

This study will determine if eyes respond differently to light in children suffering from MECP2 gene related disorders compared to neurotypical children.

¿POR QUÉ ES IMPORTANTE?

Este estudio determinará si los ojos de los niños con trastornos relacionados con el gen MECP2 responden de forma diferente a la luz en comparación con los niños sanos.

Your child may be eligible to participate if, 2-18 years of age, male, has normal vision (glasses/contact lenses are okay), the child's guardian can communicate in English or Spanish, able to travel to the Texas Medical Center.

Yes, there will be compensation of $300 per child at the end of the visits.

There are a total of 2 visits to the Texas Medical Center. The visits are separated by 3-4 months.
