Congratulations!

You and your child are one step closer to making a difference in devastating rare neurological disease research.

Usted y su hijo están muy cerca de marcar la diferencia en el conocimiento de las enfermedades neurológicas raras y devastadoras.

We are conducting a research study to measure how eyes respond to light and analyze brain health for devastating developmental brain disorders such as MECP2 Duplication Syndrome. These disorders exhibit severe intellectual disability and impaired motor function. Your participation in this research study will help us to find a treatment in the future for such rare neurological disorders that currently have no cure.
This is NOT a treatment study and there is NO medication or NO invasive procedure. There are NO appreciable risks involved in the study.

Estamos llevando a cabo un estudio para medir cómo responden los ojos a la luz y analizar la salud del cerebro cuando se padecen trastornos devastadores del desarrollo neurológico, como el Síndrome de Duplicación de MECP2. Estos trastornos presentan una discapacidad intelectual grave y una disfunción motriz. Su participación en este estudio nos ayudará en el futuro a encontrar un tratamiento para trastornos neurológicos atípicos que de momento no tienen cura.
Este estudio NO involucra tratamientos, ni medicamentos o procedimientos invasivos. Este estudio NO conlleva riesgos considerables.

WHO ARE WE SEEKING?

We are looking for healthy male children 2-18 years of age who have no history of neurological, psychiatric, or
neuro-developmental diagnosis.

¿A QUIÉNES SOLICITAMOS?

Solicitamos la participación de varones sanos de 2 a 18 años de edad sin diagnósticos psiquiátricos, neurológicos o del desarrollo neurológico.

WHAT DOES IT INVOLVE?

Your child will be presented with moderate intensity light flashes while we take video of the pupil of his eye. You and your child will also fill out behavior assessment questionnaires that will be screened by a pediatrician.

¿EN QUÉ CONSISTE?

Este estudio determinará si los ojos de los niños, que padecen trastornos relacionados con el gen MECP2, responden de diferente manera a la luz en comparación con niños que han tenido un desarrollo neurológico típico.

WHY IS IT IMPORTANT?

This study will determine if eyes respond differently to light in children suffering from MECP2 gene related disorders compared to neurotypical children.

¿POR QUÉ ES IMPORTANTE?

Este estudio determinará si los ojos de los niños, que padecen trastornos relacionados con el gen MECP2, responden de diferente manera a la luz en comparación con niños neurotípicos.

FAQ

Who can participate in this study?

Your child may be eligible to participate if, 2-18 years of age, male, has normal hearing and vision (glasses/contact lenses are Okay), the child's guardian can communicate in English or Spanish, able to travel to the Texas Medical Center.

Will I get compensated for participating in this study

Yes, there will be compensation provided at the end of each visit.

How many visits are required for this trial?

There are a total of 2 visits to the Texas Medical Center. The first visit will last for 4 hours, and the second visit will last for 2-2.5 hours. The visits are separated by 3-4 months.

Whom can I contact to get more information about this study?
